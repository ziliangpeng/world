# The Stargate Project: A Financial Deep Dive

**Research Report on Ownership, Financing, and Economic Model**

*Last Updated: November 2025*

---

## Executive Summary

The Stargate Project represents one of the most ambitious and complex AI infrastructure initiatives ever undertaken, with a stated $500 billion investment target by 2029. However, beneath the headline-grabbing figures lies a sophisticated financial structure that is fundamentally different from what most observers understand.

**Key Findings:**

- **Stargate LLC** is not a traditional datacenter company but a **cost-sharing vehicle** designed to give OpenAI access to massive AI infrastructure without bearing the full capital burden
- **Ownership is multi-layered**: Stargate LLC (owned by SoftBank/OpenAI/Oracle/MGX) leases infrastructure from Blue Owl Capital/Crusoe/Primary Digital, which Oracle operates, and OpenAI rents
- **The "$500B" is misleading**: Only ~$50B is actual equity; the rest is debt financing and asset valuation over time
- **OpenAI's actual cost**: ~$24B/year net (after profit share), not the $500B+ implied by headlines
- **Financial engineering**: SoftBank's "90% debt" model is aggressive and relies on project financing typically used in oil & gas
- **Critical uncertainties**: Bloomberg reported in August 2025 that "no funds were raised" beyond initial commitments, raising execution questions

**The Bottom Line:** Stargate allows OpenAI to access $500B in infrastructure for $19B upfront equity and ~$24B/year operating costs—compared to $500B+ if built independently. This is achieved through debt leverage, real estate partnerships, and planned revenue from other customers.

## 1. Project Overview

### What is Stargate?

Stargate LLC is an American multinational AI joint venture created by OpenAI, SoftBank, Oracle, and investment firm MGX. Announced on January 21, 2025, by President Donald Trump, the project aims to build $500 billion worth of AI infrastructure in the United States by 2029.

**Technical Specifications:**
- **Total capacity target**: 10 gigawatts (GW)
- **AI chips**: 2 million+ across all sites
- **Primary tenant**: OpenAI (anchor customer)
- **Timeline**: $100B deployment immediately, $500B by 2029

### Geographic Footprint

**Operational:**
- **Abilene, Texas (Stargate I)**: Flagship campus, parts operational as of mid-2025, 64,000 GB200 GPUs by end of 2026

**Announced Sites (September 2025):**
- Five additional U.S. locations
- Combined capacity: Nearly 7 GW planned
- Total investment across new sites: Over $400B in next 3 years

### Timeline & Milestones

| Date | Milestone |
|------|-----------|
| Jan 2025 | Project announced by Trump, OpenAI, SoftBank |
| Jun 2025 | Oracle begins delivering first GB200 racks to Abilene |

## 2. Ownership Structure: The Four Layers

Understanding Stargate requires unraveling its multi-layered ownership structure. Most analyses get this wrong by conflating the joint venture entity with the physical infrastructure.

### Layer 1: Stargate LLC (The Joint Venture Entity)

**Equity Ownership:**

| Partner | Ownership | Equity Commitment | Role |
|---------|-----------|-------------------|------|
| **SoftBank** | 40% | $19 billion | Financial responsibility, Chairman (Masa Son) |
| **OpenAI** | 40% | $19 billion | Operational responsibility |
| **Oracle** | ~10% | $7 billion | Infrastructure builder/operator |
| **MGX** | ~10% | $7 billion | Financial investor (UAE sovereign wealth) |
| **Total** | 100% | **$52 billion** | |

**Source:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2025/01/23/openai-and-softbank-are-reportedly-putting-19b-each-into-stargate/)

**Governance:**
- Masayoshi Son (SoftBank CEO): Chairman
- SoftBank: Financial responsibility
- OpenAI: Operational responsibility

**What Stargate LLC Actually Owns:**
- Customer contracts (primarily with OpenAI)
- Intellectual property and operational rights
- **NOT the physical datacenters** (this is crucial)

### Layer 2: Physical Infrastructure Owners

**The Blue Owl / Crusoe / Primary Digital Joint Venture**

The actual land, buildings, and datacenter facilities are owned by a separate joint venture consisting of:

- **Blue Owl Capital**: Asset management firm (Real Assets platform)
- **Crusoe Energy Systems**: Datacenter developer
- **Primary Digital Infrastructure**: Datacenter investment firm

**Financial Details:**
- **$15 billion** in debt and equity secured for Abilene campus (second phase)
- **$11.6 billion** financing package for eight buildings total
- **$9.6 billion** in loans from JPMorgan alone
- **Initial JV (Oct 2024)**: $3.4 billion for first phase

**Source:** [Business Wire](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20241015910376/en/Crusoe-Blue-Owl-Capital-and-Primary-Digital-Infrastructure-Enter-$3.4-billion-Joint-Venture-for-AI-Data-Center-Development)

**What They Own:**
- Land and buildings
- Power infrastructure (1.2 GW Abilene campus)
- Cooling and networking systems
- Physical datacenter shells

**Business Model:**
- Build datacenters
- **Lease to Oracle Corporation**
- Oracle operates and sublets capacity to Stargate/OpenAI

### Layer 3: Hardware & Operations (Oracle's Role)

**Oracle Corporation:**

- **Leases** datacenter facilities from Blue Owl/Crusoe/Primary Digital
- **Purchases** GPUs and servers ($40B order from NVIDIA for Abilene alone)
- **Builds out** compute infrastructure with NVIDIA partnership
- **Operates** the facilities (Oracle Cloud Infrastructure)
- **Charges** Stargate LLC for capacity

**Key Point:** Oracle is a **lessee and operator**, not the owner of the physical real estate.

**Technology Partners:**
- NVIDIA: GPU supplier, co-design of infrastructure
- Microsoft: Technology partner
- Arm: Chip architecture partner

### Layer 4: Usage Rights (OpenAI as Tenant)

**OpenAI:**
- **Pays** ~$30 billion/year to Stargate LLC
- **Receives** guaranteed capacity as "anchor tenant"
- **Owns** 40% of Stargate LLC (gets back profit share)
- **Owns** 0% of physical infrastructure

**Future Expected Tenants:**
- Microsoft (for Azure AI services)
- Other AI companies
- Enterprise customers
- Government agencies

## 3. Financial Architecture: The 90% Debt Model

### Capital Structure Overview

The "$500 billion" headline figure breaks down very differently than it appears:

**Total Project Value: $500 Billion (by 2029)**

| Component | Amount | Percentage | Nature |
|-----------|--------|------------|---------|
| Common Equity | ~$50B | 10% | Cash from investors |
| Preferred Equity + Mezzanine Debt | ~$100B | 20% | Hybrid instruments |
| Senior Debt | ~$350B | 70% | Bank loans, bonds |

**Source:** [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-02-10/softbank-explores-project-financing-for-much-of-500-billion-stargate-push)

### Phase 1: Initial Deployment ($100B)

**Initial Capital Structure:**
- **Equity**: $3 billion
- **Debt**: $10 billion
- **Debt-to-Equity Ratio**: 3:1

This ratio will expand to 9:1 (90% debt) as the project scales.

### The SoftBank Financial Engineering

**"Project Financing" Model (Oil & Gas Style):**

Traditional infrastructure projects use ~33% equity, 67% debt. SoftBank is attempting:
- **10% equity** ($50B)
- **90% debt/debt-like instruments** ($450B)

**How it works:**
1. Each datacenter site is a separate project
2. Lenders finance based on projected cash flows (OpenAI's $30B/year commitment)
3. Debt is repaid from operating revenue
4. Minimal upfront equity required from sponsors

**Challenges:**
- Extremely aggressive leverage
- Requires confident lenders
- Depends on sustained AI demand
- No comparable precedent at this scale

**Source:** [Bloomberg - SoftBank project financing](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-02-10/softbank-explores-project-financing-for-much-of-500-billion-stargate-push)

### Debt Financing Sources

**Major Debt Packages Announced:**

1. **Oracle's $38 Billion Package** (Oct 2025)
   - Led by: JPMorgan Chase, MUFG
   - Purpose: Fund Stargate datacenter expansion
   - Status: Nearing close
   - **Source:** [WinBuzzer](https://winbuzzer.com/2025/10/24/oracle-nears-record-38-billion-debt-deal-to-fund-openais-stargate-data-center-expansion-xcxwbn/)

2. **JPMorgan Abilene Loans** (2025)
   - $9.6 billion in two tranches
   - For Blue Owl/Crusoe/Primary Digital JV
   - Plus $2.3 billion additional facility
   - **Source:** [Crusoe Press Release](https://www.crusoe.ai/resources/newsroom/crusoe-blue-owl-capital-and-primary-digital-infrastructure-enter-joint-venture)

3. **SoftBank Bridge Loan** (Early 2025)
   - $16.5 billion
   - Largest bridge loan in SoftBank history
   - Short-term facility to be refinanced
   - **Source:** [S&P Global](https://www.spglobal.com/market-intelligence/en/news-insights/research/softbabnk-openai-oracle-and-mgx-commit-to-100b-for-stargate-ai-infrastructure)

**Total Debt Raised/Announced**: ~$66B publicly disclosed (target: $350-450B)

### Equity Contributions

This raises questions about whether announced commitments have actually been funded.

## 6. Financial Engineering Analysis

### Why This Complex Structure?

**Five Strategic Advantages:**

#### 1. Capital Efficiency
- OpenAI invests $19B instead of $500B
- 96% capital reduction upfront
- Access to identical infrastructure

#### 2. Risk Sharing
- Construction risk: Borne by Blue Owl/Crusoe/Primary Digital
- Demand risk: Shared across four equity partners
- Operational risk: Oracle's responsibility
- Financing risk: Lenders bear debt risk

#### 3. Asset Ownership
- OpenAI owns 40% of Stargate LLC
- If Stargate valued at $500B, OpenAI's stake worth $200B
- Can monetize via sale, IPO, or collateralization

#### 4. Cost Socialization
- Other customers' payments reduce OpenAI's burden
- If others pay $20B/year, OpenAI's effective cost drops significantly
- Multi-tenant model subsidizes anchor tenant

#### 5. Debt Leverage
- 90% debt ratio amplifies equity returns
- If project generates 10% return on $500B = $50B/year
- $50B profit on $50B equity = 100% ROI to equity holders
- (vs. 10% ROI if 100% equity-financed)

### The SoftBank Playbook

**Masa Son's Financial Engineering:**

SoftBank's approach mirrors techniques from:
- Oil & gas megaprojects (project financing)
- Real estate development (minimal equity, maximum debt)
- Telecom infrastructure (cash flow-backed lending)

**Key Innovation:**
- Use OpenAI's contractual commitment ($30B/year) as collateral
- Lenders underwrite based on guaranteed revenue stream
- Requires only 10% equity upfront

**Historical Precedent:**
- SoftBank Vision Fund: Raised $100B with ~33% from SoftBank, rest from LPs
- Sprint/T-Mobile: Heavily debt-financed
- Arm Holdings: Leveraged buyouts and refinancing

**Risk:**
This model fails if:
- AI demand collapses
- OpenAI cannot pay $30B/year
- Other customers don't materialize
- Lenders lose confidence

## 7. Critical Risks & Uncertainties

### Execution Risk: Has Capital Actually Been Raised?

**Bloomberg Report (August 2025):**
> "The project had not started and no funds were raised to meet the project's initial $500 billion budget."

**What we know:**
- **Announced**: $52B equity commitments, $66B+ debt packages
- **Actually funded**: Unclear beyond Blue Owl/Crusoe/Primary Digital's $15B for Abilene

**Questions:**
1. Has SoftBank actually transferred $19B to Stargate LLC?
2. Has OpenAI funded its $19B commitment?
3. Is the $38B Oracle debt package actually closed?

### Demand Risk: What if AI Growth Slows?

**Bear Case:**
- ChatGPT growth plateaus
- OpenAI revenue stays at $13-20B/year
- Cannot afford $30B/year Stargate payments
- Defaults on contract
- Stargate's debt becomes unsustainable

**Impact on stakeholders:**
- Lenders: $350B at risk
- SoftBank/OpenAI: Equity wiped out
- Oracle: Loses infrastructure customer
- Blue Owl/Crusoe: Tenants default on leases

### Debt Burden Risk

**$350 Billion in senior debt** requires:
- Annual interest: ~$10-14B (at 3-4% rates)
- Must be serviced even if revenue disappoints
- Covenants likely require minimum revenue/EBITDA

**Comparison:**
- U.S. corporate debt market: ~$10T total
- Stargate's $350B = 3.5% of entire U.S. corporate debt market
- This is enormous and unprecedented for a single project

### SoftBank Capacity Risk

**Can SoftBank actually fund this?**

- SoftBank market cap: ~$80-100B
- Stargate commitment: $19B
- SoftBank's other commitments: Vision Fund 2, other investments
- Recent report: Seeking $16.5B bridge loan (need to borrow to fund commitment)

**Red flags:**
- If SoftBank needs to borrow to meet its equity commitment, is it truly equity?
- Can they sustain this if markets turn?

### Counterparty Risk: Blue Owl/Crusoe/Primary Digital

**Reliance on JV partners:**
- Must successfully build datacenters
- Must secure power (1.2 GW+ per site)
- Must deliver on time
- Any delays impact entire project

**Track record:**
- Crusoe: Proven in AI datacenter buildouts
- Blue Owl: $200B+ AUM, strong backing
- Primary Digital: Newer entity, less track record

## 8. Comparison: xAI vs. OpenAI Infrastructure Strategies

### xAI's Direct Ownership Model

**Colossus 1 & 2:**
- Current: 230,000 GPUs operational (H100/H200/GB200)
- Target: 1,000,000+ GPUs by late 2025/early 2026
- Location: Memphis, Tennessee (2 facilities)

**Financial Model:**
- **Capital approach**: Direct purchase of GPUs and infrastructure
- **Spending**: $18B+ for 300,000 GPU order
- **Annual operating cost**: $5-10B/year (own vs. rent)
- **Buildout speed**: 122 days for first 100,000 GPU cluster

**Advantages:**
- Lower long-term cost (buy vs. rent)
- Full control over infrastructure
- Fastest deployment (4 months vs. 12-24 months for cloud providers)
- Own power generation (Southaven plant)

**Disadvantages:**
- Higher capital intensity upfront ($18B+ vs. OpenAI's $19B for access to $500B)
- Concentrated in single location (Memphis)
- Must manage operations internally

### OpenAI's Hybrid Model

**Multi-Cloud + Stargate:**
- Stargate: 40% ownership, ~$24B/year net cost
- Microsoft Azure: ~$2-10B/year
- AWS: ~$5.4B/year
- CoreWeave: ~$7.5B/year
- Google Cloud: ~$1-2B/year

**Total Spending**: $40-55B/year at peak

**Advantages:**
- Geographic distribution (risk mitigation)
- Multiple vendors prevent lock-in
- Can scale up/down flexibly
- Don't manage infrastructure directly

**Disadvantages:**
- Much higher annual costs ($40-55B vs. xAI's $5-10B)
- Coordination complexity (managing 5+ relationships)
- Still dependent on partners' buildout timelines
- Paying cloud markup

### Strategic Comparison

| Dimension | xAI | OpenAI |
|-----------|-----|--------|
| **Philosophy** | Own everything, control destiny | Outsource infrastructure, focus on AI |
| **Capital Efficiency** | Lower long-term, higher upfront | Lower upfront, higher long-term |
| **Speed** | Fastest (4 months to deploy 100K) | Slower (dependent on partners) |
| **Risk** | Concentrated (single location) | Distributed (multi-cloud) |
| **Annual Cost** | $5-10B | $40-55B |
| **Total GPUs Accessible** | ~1M (owned) | ~2M+ (rented via Stargate + clouds) |

**Key Insight:**
- xAI optimizes for **cost and control**
- OpenAI optimizes for **scale and risk mitigation**

Which is better depends on:
- Available capital
- Risk tolerance
- Speed requirements
- Long-term vision (own infrastructure vs. infrastructure-as-a-service)

## 11. Conclusions

### Key Findings

1. **Stargate is a cost-sharing vehicle, not a traditional profit-making enterprise**
   - Primary purpose: Give OpenAI access to $500B infrastructure for $19B equity + $24B/year costs
   - Success measured by cost reduction, not profit maximization

2. **Multi-layered ownership creates complexity but spreads risk**
   - Stargate LLC (SoftBank/OpenAI 80%) owns contracts, not physical assets
   - Blue Owl/Crusoe/Primary Digital own real estate
   - Oracle operates as lessee
   - OpenAI rents as tenant

3. **The "$500B" figure is primarily debt and asset valuation**
   - Only $50-52B actual equity
   - $350-450B debt (unprecedented scale)
   - Success depends on raising this debt at 90% leverage ratio

4. **OpenAI's economics make sense IF revenue grows massively**
   - Current revenue: $13B/year
   - Required spending: $40-55B/year (Stargate + other clouds)
   - Must grow 3-4x in revenue within 2-3 years
   - Otherwise, unsustainable without continuous investor funding

5. **SoftBank's financial engineering is aggressive and risky**
   - 90% debt ratio is extreme
   - Project financing model unproven at this scale
   - Execution risk: Bloomberg reported "no funds raised" (Aug 2025)
   - SoftBank may be over-leveraged

6. **Other customers are essential for profitability**
   - If only OpenAI pays $30B, Stargate loses money
   - Need $20-70B from other tenants
   - Not guaranteed they will materialize

7. **Comparison to xAI reveals strategic trade-offs**
   - xAI: Lower cost ($5-10B/year), faster (4 months), more control, higher capital intensity
   - OpenAI: Higher cost ($40-55B/year), slower (partner-dependent), distributed risk, lower upfront capital

### The Ultimate Question: Will It Work?

**Bull Case (Optimistic):**
- AI demand continues explosive growth
- OpenAI revenue reaches $50-100B/year by 2027-2029
- Other customers (Microsoft, Meta, etc.) rent $20-50B/year capacity
- SoftBank successfully raises $350B debt
- Blue Owl/Crusoe/Primary Digital deliver on time
- Stargate becomes profitable, OpenAI's 40% stake worth $200B+
- **Everyone wins**

**Bear Case (Pessimistic):**
- AI growth slows or plateaus
- OpenAI revenue stuck at $15-20B/year
- Cannot afford $30B/year Stargate payments
- Other customers build their own infrastructure (like xAI)
- SoftBank cannot raise $350B at 90% leverage
- Debt becomes unsustainable
- Defaults cascade through system
- **Everyone loses (except maybe lenders with security)**

**Most Likely Case (Realistic):**
- Stargate completes 2-3 datacenter sites, not full 6-8
- Total investment: $150-250B, not $500B
- OpenAI's revenue grows to $30-40B/year (enough to pay commitment)
- Some other customers materialize ($10-20B/year)
- SoftBank uses more equity, less debt (60-70% debt, not 90%)
- Project is marginally profitable
- OpenAI gets infrastructure it needs at manageable cost
- **Moderate success, not transformational**

### The Bottom Line

**OpenAI doesn't "have" $500 billion or $1.4 trillion.**

Through sophisticated financial structuring involving:
- Joint venture equity (40% ownership)
- Debt leverage (targeting 90%)
- Real estate partnerships (Blue Owl/Crusoe/Primary Digital)
- Operational outsourcing (Oracle)
- Revenue sharing (profit distribution)

OpenAI has created a path to massive AI infrastructure with:
- **$19B upfront equity** (vs. $500B if building alone)
- **~$24B/year net cost** (vs. $130B/year if building alone)
- **40% ownership** of valuable infrastructure assets

**This is financial engineering at its finest—or most fragile, depending on execution.**

The model works IF:
1. AI demand sustains
2. OpenAI revenue grows 3-4x
3. Other customers pay $20-70B/year
4. SoftBank raises $350B debt
5. Blue Owl/Crusoe/Primary Digital deliver
6. Oracle executes operations flawlessly

**That's a lot of "ifs."**
